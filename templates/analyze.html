Glossary — what the numbers mean

Accuracy & bias

Mean miss The tracked accuracy metric: the average distance of each arrow from the gold. It counts both a group that sits off-centre and a loose one, so a wide spray can't read as accurate.
Bias (MPI) Mean Point of Impact: magnitude of the group's centroid offset from the bullseye — the group's bias, i.e. which way to move the sight or anchor. Not the same as accuracy: a loose group centred on the gold has near-zero bias but a large mean miss.
Bias Δx, Δy Signed offset of the centroid. The direction tells you which way to move the sight, not just by how much.

Precision — how tight the group is

R95 Radius about the group's own centroid that contains 95% of its shots. The headline precision number — independent of where the centroid sits. (Below 10 shots it's an empirical percentile rather than a Rayleigh fit, so treat it as approximate.)
Mean Radius (MR) Average distance of each shot from the group's own centroid. Secondary precision number, canonical in shooting-sport testing.
σ_x / σ_y Per-axis standard deviation about the centroid. Large σ_y with small σ_x is vertical stringing (release or anchor variation); the reverse is horizontal stringing (bow torque or grip).
Extreme spread (ES) Largest pairwise distance between any two shots in the group. Familiar from firearm and 3D archery scoring — sensitive to one bad shot.

Head-to-head tests

Hotelling's T² On the 2D shot vectors: tests whether two groups' centroids sit in different places — a bias difference, independent of how tight either group is.
Brown-Forsythe F On distance-from-each-group's-own-centroid: tests whether one group clusters more tightly than the other — pure precision, independent of bias.
Mann-Whitney U On distance-from-target-center: a practical tiebreaker — does one setup simply land closer to the bull on average? It can't say why (bias vs spread); use the accuracy / precision rows for that.
Cliff's δ Effect size paired with Mann-Whitney. Range −1…+1. Positive means this group's distances tend to exceed the other's (the other lands closer); 0 means no ordering.
Holm-Bonferroni Step-down p-value adjustment applied within each test family (accuracy / precision / total error), so testing many pairs doesn't inflate false positives beyond α.
p-value / α Results are called at α = 0.05 on Holm-adjusted p-values. Small samples often can't reject the null even when a real effect exists, so a null result reads "inconclusive," not "no difference" — treat as exploratory.
