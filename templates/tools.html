Tools

Wind drift

Drag-based lateral drift on a cylindrical shaft. Drift scales with wind², so a gust catching the arrow mid-flight drifts disproportionately more than the mean wind suggests. Heavier and skinnier shafts drift less. Fletching weathercocks the arrow back toward the apparent wind in flight, so real drift is typically 30–60% of this estimate.

—

Wind angle: 0° = headwind/tailwind (no drift), 90° = pure crosswind. Set peak gust = mean wind for a steady-wind result. Real archery wind is typically gust-factor 1.4–1.7 (peak ÷ mean).

Sight marks

Enter two or more known sight settings, then ask for any other distance. Uses piecewise linear interpolation, extrapolates with the nearest segment's slope.

—

Distance and mark can be in any units you like — the calculator just interpolates within the column you entered.

Arrow spine selector

Rough recommendation from your bow's draw weight, arrow length, point weight, and bow type. Always cross-check with the arrow manufacturer's chart before buying a dozen.

—

Adjusted bow weight = draw weight + 5 lbs per inch over 28″ + 1.5 lbs per 25 gr over 100 gr point weight, with a small correction for compound vs recurve vs longbow.

FOC — front-of-center

FOC% = how far the balance point sits forward of the arrow's geometric center, as a percentage of total length.

—

Bands: <7% low · 7–9% target · 10–15% hunting · 15–30% EFOC. Measure total length from the throat of the nock to the back of the point.

Arrow speed (fps)

Two methods. Use IBO/ATA if you already know your bow's advertised speed (compound-only). Use bow specs for an energy-storage estimate that works for any bow type — recurve, compound, or longbow — from physical parameters alone.

—

Energy model: stored energy ≈ k·Fpeak·stroke (k accounts for the force-draw curve shape, including let-off on compounds), then η·Estored goes into the arrow as kinetic energy. Recurve k=0.45/η=0.72, compound k=0.65/η=0.85, longbow k=0.40/η=0.70. Always trust a chronograph over a calculator.

Adjustments: −2 fps per lb under rated weight · −10 fps per inch under rated draw · −1 fps per 3 gr over rated arrow weight · −1 fps per 3 gr of string accessory weight. Note: IBO and ATA both standardize 70 lb / 30″ / 350 gr — the two ratings give identical numbers here.

Kinetic energy & momentum

Energy delivered at the chronograph point, plus momentum (a better predictor of penetration than KE for big-game hunters).

—

KE (ft·lb) = mass × velocity² ÷ 450,240. Momentum (slug·ft/s) = mass × velocity ÷ 225,400.
